Ministries

Find your place to serve. Every member has a gift, and every gift matters in the body of Christ.

At Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church, we believe that every member of the body of Christ has been uniquely gifted by the Holy Spirit for service. Our ministries exist to help you discover your gifts, develop your skills, and deploy them for the glory of God and the blessing of others. Whether you are drawn to worship, teaching, evangelism, mercy, administration, or creative arts, there is a place for you to make a meaningful impact.

Each of our ministries is led by dedicated servant-leaders who are passionate about equipping others and building up the church. We provide regular training, mentorship, and support to help every volunteer thrive in their area of service. Explore the ministries below and take the next step in finding your place to serve at Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church!

Join a Ministry Today

Getting involved in a ministry is one of the best ways to grow in your faith, build meaningful relationships, and make a difference in the lives of others. You don't need to have everything figured out — you just need a willing heart and a desire to serve.

Here is how to get started: First, pray and ask God where He is leading you. Second, browse the ministries above and identify 2-3 that interest you. Third, fill out a Ministry Interest Form at the church office or speak to any ministry leader after a Sunday service. Someone from the ministry team will follow up with you within one week to help you take the next step.

Volunteer Opportunities

Not sure where to start? Consider one of these essential volunteer roles that keep our church running and our community served.

Usher

Every Sunday · 1 hour before service

Ushers are the first friendly faces people see when they arrive at church. Responsibilities include greeting arrivals, helping people find seats, distributing bulletins, managing the flow of people during communion, and assisting with offerings. Ushers serve on a rotating schedule, typically one Sunday per month.

Choir Member

Wednesday 5 PM · Sunday 7 AM

Our choir leads the congregation in worship every Sunday morning and at special events. Choir members rehearse on Wednesday evenings and Sunday mornings before the service. An audition is required, and basic music reading ability is preferred but not required. A heart for worship is the most important qualification.

Sunday School Teacher

Every Sunday · 2 hours/week

Sunday School teachers invest in the spiritual formation of the next generation by preparing and delivering age-appropriate Bible lessons, leading activities, and building relationships with children. Training and curriculum are provided. A background check and 3-month mentoring period are required for all new teachers.

Media Team

Every Sunday · 3 hours/week

The media team handles live video recording, live-streaming, sound engineering, projection, and social media content creation during services and events. Technical training is provided for all equipment. This role is ideal for those who are tech-savvy or want to learn multimedia production skills.

Outreach Volunteer

Monthly events · 4-6 hours/month

Outreach volunteers participate in community service projects including food distribution, health fairs, neighborhood cleanups, prison visits, and evangelistic campaigns. Volunteers serve on a project-by-project basis and can choose which events to participate in based on their schedule and interests.

Food Bank Helper

Thursdays · 3 hours/week

Food bank volunteers sort donations, pack food parcels, register beneficiaries, and distribute food packages to families in need every Thursday. The food bank serves over 200 families monthly and is one of our most impactful community ministries. No special skills are needed, just a compassionate heart and willing hands.

Ministry Leadership Training

At Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church, we believe that every ministry leader should be equipped not only with practical skills but also with deep spiritual formation and biblical knowledge. That is why we have established our quarterly Ministry Leadership Training program, designed to raise up leaders who are competent, Christ-like, and committed to the mission of the church.

The training program covers a comprehensive curriculum that includes biblical foundations of leadership, servant leadership principles, conflict resolution, team building, communication skills, and spiritual disciplines. Each quarter focuses on a different theme, and participants work through both classroom instruction and practical ministry projects.

The program is open to all current and aspiring ministry leaders and volunteers. Sessions are held on the first Saturday of each quarter from 9:00 AM to 3:00 PM. Meals and materials are provided free of charge. Upon completion of the four-quarter cycle, participants receive a Ministry Leadership Certificate and are commissioned for service.
