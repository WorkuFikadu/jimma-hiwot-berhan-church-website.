First Visit Guide

We want your first visit to be as smooth and enjoyable as possible. Here is everything you need to know before you come.

Parking & Arrival

  • Free parking is available on the church grounds with space for over 100 vehicles. Overflow parking is available at the adjacent community center lot.
  • Our main entrance faces Jimma-Bishoftu Road. Look for the green church sign at the gate.
  • Greeters will be at the entrance from 7:30 AM on Sundays to welcome you and direct you to the sanctuary.
  • If you have mobility needs, accessible parking spaces are available near the main entrance, and our building is wheelchair accessible with ramps and designated seating areas.

What to Wear

  • Come as you are! There is no dress code at Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church. Whether you prefer traditional Ethiopian attire, casual clothes, or business wear, you will fit right in.
  • Most of our members dress modestly and comfortably. For the traditional Sunday morning service, some members wear white traditional clothing (netela or gabi), but this is entirely optional.
  • The most important thing is not what you wear on the outside, but the condition of your heart. We care about you, not your clothes.

Children's Check-in

  • Children's check-in opens 20 minutes before each Sunday service. Visit the Children's Ministry desk in the lobby to register your child.
  • You will receive a matching parent tag and child tag with a unique code. This ensures only you can pick up your child after the service.
  • Classes are divided by age: Nursery (0-2 years), Toddlers (3-4 years), Pre-K to Kindergarten (5-6 years), and Primary (7-12 years).
  • All classrooms are clean, safe, and staffed by trained volunteers who love children and are passionate about teaching them about God's love.

Find Us

Address

Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church, Jimma Town, Oromia Region, Ethiopia. Located on Jimma-Bishoftu Road, approximately 2 km from Jimma city center, near the Jimma University main campus.

Directions from Jimma City Center

From the Jimma city center (near the main bus station), head east on Bishoftu Road for approximately 2 kilometers. You will see our church on the right side of the road, just past Jimma University main campus. The journey takes about 8 minutes by car or 25 minutes on foot. Public minibuses (Route 5) pass directly in front of the church.

Parking

Free parking is available on-site for over 100 vehicles, including accessible parking spaces near the main entrance. Overflow parking is available at the adjacent community center. Parking attendants will guide you to available spaces on busy Sundays.

Special Services

Throughout the year, we hold special worship services to celebrate key moments in the Christian calendar. These services are deeply meaningful times of worship, reflection, and celebration.

Christmas Celebration

December 25 (Genna)

Our Christmas service is a joyous celebration of the birth of Jesus Christ. The service features special choral performances by our adult and children's choirs, a dramatic nativity reenactment, candlelight worship, and a powerful Christmas message from our Senior Pastor. The service begins at 6:00 AM on Christmas morning, followed by a communal feast where all members and visitors share food and fellowship together.

Easter Sunrise Service

Date Varies (Fasika)

Our Easter celebration begins with a sunrise service at 5:00 AM in the church courtyard, symbolizing the empty tomb and the resurrection of our Lord. The service includes hymns of victory, a resurrection message, and a time of praise. Following the sunrise service, we hold our main Easter worship at 9:00 AM with special music, baptisms, and a celebratory message on the power of the resurrection.

Good Friday Service

Date Varies (Siklet)

Our Good Friday service is a solemn and reflective time as we remember the sacrifice of Jesus Christ on the cross. Held at 3:00 PM, the service includes the reading of the passion narrative, guided prayers of confession and repentance, reflective worship songs, a message on the meaning of the cross, and a time of communion. This deeply moving service prepares our hearts for the joy of Easter Sunday.
