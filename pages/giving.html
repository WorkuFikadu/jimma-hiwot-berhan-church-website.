In Person

You can give during any of our worship services by placing your tithe or offering in the offering box located at the entrance of the sanctuary. Offering envelopes are available at the Welcome Desk for those who wish to use them.

For large donations or special gifts, we encourage you to visit the church office during business hours (Monday-Saturday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM) where our finance team can assist you and provide a receipt.

Offering boxes are opened and counted by at least two authorized finance team members following strict financial procedures.

Online Giving

Tax Deductible Receipts

All donations to Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church are eligible for tax deduction under Ethiopian law. If you would like to receive an official donation receipt for tax purposes, please visit the church office during business hours (Monday-Saturday, 8:00 AM - 5:00 PM) with your identification and details of your contributions.

Annual contribution statements are mailed to all regular givers in January of each year. If you have not received yours or need an additional copy, please contact our finance office at finance@hiwotberhan.org or call +251 47 111 2233.

Frequently Asked Questions

A tithe is the practice of giving the first 10% of your income back to God as an act of obedience and worship (Leviticus 27:30, Proverbs 3:9-10). An offering is any gift given above and beyond the tithe, often given in response to a specific need, a special occasion, or as a voluntary expression of gratitude. Both are biblical and both are acts of worship that honor God and support the work of the church.

Our finances are managed by a dedicated Finance Committee made up of church elders, deacons, and members with professional accounting experience. All funds are handled according to strict financial policies and procedures. The church undergoes an annual external audit by a licensed auditing firm, and a summary report is presented to the congregation at our annual general meeting. We are committed to the highest standards of financial integrity and transparency.

We understand that financial seasons vary, and we never want anyone to give out of guilt or pressure. Giving is meant to be a joyful act of worship, not a burden. If you are going through a difficult financial season, we encourage you to focus on trusting God with your situation and give according to what you are able, not what you feel pressured to give. If you need practical financial assistance, please speak with our pastoral team — we are here to support you in prayer and through our deacon's benevolence fund.
