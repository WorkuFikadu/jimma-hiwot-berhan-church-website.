About Our Church

Discover the story, faith, and people that make Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church a place of light and life in Jimma, Ethiopia.

Who We Are

Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church, which translates to "Light of Life Church," was founded in 1948 E.C (1956 G.C.) by Pastor Tesfaye Hailu and a small group of devoted believers who shared a vision of establishing a Christ-centered community in the heart of Jimma, Oromia, Ethiopia. What began as a humble gathering of just twelve people has grown, by God's grace, into a thriving congregation of over 2,500 members.

For over 70 years, Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church has been a spiritual home for people from all walks of life — students, families, business owners, farmers, and professionals — united by a common faith in Jesus Christ and a commitment to loving God and loving others. Our name reflects our deep conviction that Jesus Christ is the Light of Life (John 8:12), and we are called to reflect that light in our community.

Today, our church is more than just a place of Sunday worship. We are a vibrant, multi-generational community that gathers throughout the week for prayer, Bible study, youth activities, women's fellowships, men's meetings, music rehearsals, and community outreach. We have built strong partnerships with local schools, health clinics, and charitable organizations to make a tangible difference in the lives of those around us.

Our story is not one of buildings and programs, but of transformed lives. We have seen marriages restored, addictions broken, young people finding their purpose, and the hopeless finding renewed faith. Every testimony in our church is a testament to the faithfulness of God and the power of the Gospel. As we look to the future, we remain committed to our founding vision: to be a beacon of hope, a school of discipleship, and a force for good in Jimma and beyond.

Our Core Values

These six core values guide every decision, program, and relationship at Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church.

Faith

We believe in the power of faith — faith in God who is able to do immeasurably more than all we ask or imagine. We trust His Word, rely on His promises, and step out boldly in obedience, knowing that He who began a good work in us will carry it to completion.

Love

Love is the greatest commandment and the defining mark of a follower of Christ. We strive to love God with all our heart, soul, mind, and strength, and to love our neighbors as ourselves — sacrificially, unconditionally, and without prejudice.

Unity

We are one body with many parts, called to live in harmony and mutual respect. We celebrate our diversity in age, background, and gifts while maintaining the unity of the Spirit through the bond of peace. Together we are stronger.

Service

Jesus came not to be served, but to serve. Following His example, we view service as a privilege and a calling. Whether within the church or in the wider community, we look for opportunities to use our time, talents, and resources to bless others in the name of Christ.

Excellence

Whatever we do, we do it as unto the Lord. We pursue excellence not for recognition, but as an expression of our reverence for God and our respect for those we serve. From worship to administration, we are committed to giving our very best.

Integrity

We are committed to honesty, transparency, and accountability in all that we do. Our words match our actions, and we handle God's resources and the trust placed in us with the utmost care and responsibility. Integrity is the foundation of our witness.

Our History Timeline

A journey of faith, growth, and God's faithfulness through the decades.

1948

The Church is Founded (1948 E.C.)

Pastor Tesfaye Hailu, together with a small group of believers, held the first worship service seventy years ago (1948 E.C.) in Jimma. What began as a humble gathering has grown through God's faithfulness into a thriving community.

1998

First Church Building

After years of faithful giving and sacrificial contributions from the congregation, Jimma Hiwot Berhan Church purchased its first piece of land and constructed a modest but beautiful church building. The dedication service was attended by over 300 members and guests.

2005

Youth Ministry Launch

Recognizing the need to invest in the next generation, we officially launched the Youth Ministry. Starting with 25 young people, it quickly grew through dynamic worship services, sports activities, and mentorship programs.

2012

Major Church Expansion

With the congregation growing, we embarked on a major expansion. The new facility included a larger sanctuary seating 1,500, a dedicated youth hall, children's classrooms, and administrative offices.

2020

Community Center Opened

We opened our Community Center, fulfilling a long-held dream. The center provides tutoring for underprivileged children, vocational training, a health clinic, and a food bank serving over 200 families.

Statement of Faith

We hold to the essential truths of the Christian faith as revealed in the Bible. These beliefs form the foundation of our teaching, worship, and community life.

We believe the Bible is the inspired, infallible, and authoritative Word of God. It is without error in all it affirms and is the supreme and final authority in all matters of faith and conduct. We believe that God has preserved His Word through the centuries and that it is relevant and powerful for every generation. Scripture alone is our ultimate guide for faith and practice.
We believe in one God, eternally existing in three Persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit. God is the Creator and Sustainer of all things, infinite in power, wisdom, and goodness. He is holy, righteous, loving, and faithful. He is sovereign over all creation and is working all things together for the good of those who love Him and are called according to His purpose.
We believe in the deity of the Lord Jesus Christ — His virgin birth, His sinless life, His miracles, His atoning death on the cross, His bodily resurrection, His ascension to the right hand of the Father, and His personal return in power and glory. Jesus Christ is the only mediator between God and humanity, and salvation is found in Him alone. He is the Way, the Truth, and the Life.
We believe in the present ministry of the Holy Spirit, by whose indwelling the Christian is enabled to live a godly life. The Holy Spirit convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment; regenerates and baptizes believers into the body of Christ; seals them for the day of redemption; empowers them for service; and produces spiritual fruit in their lives. We believe in the ongoing work of the Spirit through spiritual gifts for the edification of the church.
We believe that salvation is a gift of God received by faith alone in Jesus Christ alone. All people have sinned and fall short of God's glory. Through the death and resurrection of Jesus Christ, God provided the way of salvation. Those who repent of their sins and trust in Christ as Lord and Savior are forgiven, declared righteous, adopted into God's family, and given eternal life. This salvation is entirely by God's grace, not by human effort.
We believe the Church is the body of Christ, composed of all born-again believers regardless of race, ethnicity, or social status. The local church is God's primary instrument for worship, evangelism, discipleship, fellowship, and service. We observe two ordinances: water baptism as a public declaration of faith, and the Lord's Supper as a remembrance of Christ's sacrifice. We are committed to the unity of the Spirit and the bond of peace.
